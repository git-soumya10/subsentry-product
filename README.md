SubSentry – Subscription Management & Alerting Tool

Overview:

SubSentry AI is a subscription management and alerting product designed to help users track recurring subscriptions and avoid unexpected charges. The product focuses on timely reminders, clarity, and simplicity to build trust and improve user control over recurring expenses.

Problem Statement:

Many users forget about active subscriptions and only realize charges after payments are deducted. This leads to financial frustration, loss of trust, and difficulty managing recurring expenses across multiple platforms.

User Research & Discovery:

The problem was identified through personal finance pain points and informal user conversations, revealing that users primarily need:

Visibility into active subscriptions

Advance reminders before renewals

A simple, low-effort way to stay informed

Solution:

SubSentry provides a centralized view of subscriptions and sends timely alerts before renewals. The product is designed to minimize cognitive load while ensuring users never miss a renewal date.

Key Features:

Subscription tracking and listing

Renewal and payment alerts

Simple dashboard for visibility

Reminder-focused user experience

Product Scope & MVP:

Defined MVP to focus on alerts and visibility rather than advanced analytics

Prioritized features based on user value and ease of adoption

Deferred advanced features to avoid complexity in early versions

User Flow:

Add Subscription → Track Renewal Date → Receive Alert → Take Action (Continue or Cancel)

Execution:

Created wireframes and user flows to define core interactions

Wrote product requirements to guide MVP development

Focused on usability, clarity, and habit-forming reminders

Product Thinking:

Designed for trust and reliability over feature richness

Considered future monetization paths such as premium reminders and analytics

Evaluated expansion opportunities including spend insights and subscription trends

Outcomes:

Delivered a functional MVP demonstrating end-to-end product ownership

Validated the importance of reminders as the primary value driver

Showcased prioritization and scope discipline in product execution

Learnings:

Importance of solving one strong user problem well

Trade-offs between feature depth and usability

Designing products that fit naturally into user habits
